Overview

In this project, I worked on implementing a real-time simulation of cloth using masses and springs, handling collisions with itself and other objects, and applying shading techniques and texture mapping.

To build the cloth, I constructed springs that connected point masses of different types (structural, shearing, and bending) and used Verlet integration to calculate the displacement of point masses when forces are applied.

To handle collisions with other objects such as spheres, we check for any intersection between point mass displacement and the sphere, and adjust the position of point masses such that they’re slightly outside of the sphere. To handle self-collisions efficiently, I build a hashmap in which point masses within the same 3D box partition of the space hash to the same value, and then correct point mass positions if they’re too close.


I also implement diffuse and Blinn-Phong shading using the equations taught in class. I also implemented texture mapping by sampling a texture function. I also implement bump mapping by sampling the heights of a texture per fragment and adjusting the normals of the surface such that the light makes it look like it has bumps. For displacement, I corrected the vertex positions of the geometry to match the sampled texture heights as well, such that the actual surface is bumpy.

Part 5

A shader program is usually a small program that runs on the GPU that renders graphics data. The vertex shader processes the 3D geometry and passes per-vertex information such as normals down the pipeline. The fragment shader operates on each fragment (basically pixel) and is responsible for calculating the final color of that fragment; this is where more detailed visual effects can be applied like light diffusion or texture mapping.

While not a perfectly accurate model, the Blinn-Phong shading model attempts to estimate the light off of a surface by combining three sources: a set ambient light value, light diffusion, and specular highlights.

The difference between displacement and bump is that bump is much smoother since even though the light makes it look like there are protrusions in the cloth, the actual geometry is just a smooth cloth. Conversely, since displacement adjusts the cloth vertices to reflect the result of sampling the texture heights, displacement looks much more bumpier. You can see this most clearly when you observe the flat cloth hovering above the sphere.

The bump shader doesn’t really change when the coarseness of the mesh is decreased, but the quality of the displacement shader increases.
